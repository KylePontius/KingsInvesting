Overview

ZIM Integrated Shipping Services provides container shipping to clients across the world. Its chartered fleet (with a prominent amount being from DAC) is optimized to go any route that a customer desires. It currently operates a fleet of 101 ships and was found in Israel in 1945, where it is still based.

Fundamental Analysis

Similar to that of DAC, ZIM is practically printing cash due to the macroeconomic situation and supply chain congestion happening across the world. This crisis has caused companies to turn to ZIM, who specifically traverses routes which are less popular to expedite shipping. They have consistently beat all estimates since their IPO in Janurary. Furthermore, DAC has a large stake in ZIM, showing established interest and support in the company. However, this era of prosperity may not last very much longer. Recently, a ZIM operated DAC ship had recently caught fire off the coast of Canada, sparking backlash and giving bad public reputation towards the company. Furthermore, spot rates has recently fallen drastically despite a rise in the Harpex Index, insinuating that Chinese company interests in the prime shipping path between LA and Bejing may becoming to an end, leaving ZIM with less profits.

Simple Technical Analysis

ZIM has made a jump it its support-resistance channel, with no clear view to which direction it will go next.

Advanced Technical Analysis

It is reasonable to assume that ZIM is in the middle of a wave 3 of 5 on the macro view. On the micro view, it can be seen that ZIM is most likely in a wave 3 of 3 of 5. Typically, once the macro wave 4 of 5 completes, a swing entry would be reasonable to consider, however the low volume that this stock makes any technical analysis unreliable.

Thesis

ZIM is given an Analyst Rank of 6, or neutral as despite mainly great fundamentals, investor sentiment is just not present. Such low volume makes the position susceptible to unseen sudden movements, making technical analysis less reliable. If ZIM is to stock-split in a preferable 1-4 ratio, the stock would be given a rating of 9. However, the lack of volume and possible future problems pertaining to spot rates and container prices makes the stock too complex of a position to consider at the moment.
