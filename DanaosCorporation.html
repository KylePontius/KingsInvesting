Overview

Danaos Corporation is the tenth biggest container ship owner who operates and leases ships in their fleet. They have a total of 65 containerships, capable of collectively carrying 403,793 TEU. The company is based in Piraeus, Greece, and has been a company since 1972.

Fundamental Analysis

DAC is an absolute cash cow, especially with the horrid macroecnomics in the supply chain. Once Covid-19 had begun infecting much of the world, key components of maintaining the supply chain were immensely slowed or halted. This in turn caused companies to pay higher fees to shipping companies in order to preserve a slot to ship their product to their desired port. Although this did not directly effect DAC, it caused shipping companies to charter most of their fleet, with 90% of them being ordered through the end of 2022. Furthermore, DAC has consistently beat its EPS and revenue expecations in the last year by miles. However, there is one critical issue with Danaos, the age of its ship are starting to grow out of the average lifetime for container ships, which may cause fuel efficiency and condition problems. However, salvaging prices are at incredibly high prices, which could be a good investment for the company if they do choose to scrap much of their outdated fleet. A possible upbringing in the company can be seen in its large stake in ZIM, who have proven to be an absolute cash cow since their IPO due to the supply chain crisis and dividend, which DAC benefits from.

Simple Technical Analysis

DAC has broken its support line on the one year daily chart, and two year daily chart, while holding a steady RSI and consolidation between $70 and $73 marks. Volume has also been dwindling since the rally in September.

Advanced Technical Analysis

In September, DAC bounced off of the 423% extension, signaling the end of a five wave rally with a possible a wave down, needing a b-c, applying on all time frames greater than one year for weekly charts. Furthermore, another five wave structure can be seen at the beginning of 4/21/21, peaking at the 200% extension, supporting the call for an a-b-c down on the yearly charts.

Thesis

As of now, Danaos Corporation is given a rating of sell, or 4/10. DAC may be a fundamentally decent company in its current state, but months or a year from now this company and its stock could truly falter. It has an aging fleet and is not adding/replacing/salvaging much of their fleet at a rate to outpace their invevitable aging. Furthermore, after the fifth wave completion in September, volume has been dead while the stock has dropped $15+.
